About this series

Like many other photographers I have a deep love for analogue photography. Shooting a roll of film is an entirely different experience from the digital approach. To me, the fascinating thing in film is the process and journey that happens before you're able to see what you've captured. In the digital world this excitment and novelty is taken away by screens and live previews.


What if there was a way to capture that same excitment and surprise while mantaining the mathematical precision that only a CMOS sensor can give? Through my experience as a software developer I've creared my own little programs that edit my pictures by applying filters or other algorithms an introduce an element of randomness decided by the computer. Just like when a piece of film is bathed in acids and slight changes in the temperatures of these liquids can affect the final result, I wanted to replicate the same exact thing. Give up some control over the final look of my photos and give it to a machine
